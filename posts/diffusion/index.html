Go through Diffusion

Published: 17 Aug, 2025 |  at  04:30 AM
| Suggest Changes

0. Introduction

Diffusion models have emerged as one of the most powerful generative modeling frameworks in recent years, producing state-of-the-art image, video, and audio samples.
At first glance, their training objective looks deceptively simple: predict Gaussian noise with MSE loss.

But how does this connect to maximum likelihood estimation (MLE), which is the standard principle behind autoregressive models, VAEs, and normalizing flows?

We’ll walk through the full derivation: from log-likelihood → ELBO → Gaussian posteriors → noise-prediction loss.

1. Notation and Setup

  • Data: (x0∼pdata(x0))v.(x_0 \sim p_{\text{data}}(x_0))v. (x0​∼pdata​(x0​))v.
  • Noise schedule: ({\beta_t\}_{t=1}^T) , with (αt=1−βt) (\alpha_t = 1-\beta_t)(αt​=1−βt​) , and (αˉt=∏s=1tαs).(\bar\alpha_t = \prod_{s=1}^t \alpha_s).(αˉt​=∏s=1t​αs​).

2. Forward Diffusion (Noising Process)

We define a Markov chain that progressively adds Gaussian noise:

q(xt∣xt−1)=N(αt xt−1, βtI).q(x_t | x_{t-1}) = \mathcal N(\sqrt{\alpha_t}\,x_{t-1}, \, \beta_t I). q(xt​∣xt−1​)=N(αt​​xt−1​,βt​I).

Chained together:

q(x1:T∣x0)=∏t=1Tq(xt∣xt−1). q(x_{1:T} \mid x_0) = \prod_{t=1}^T q(x_t \mid x_{t-1}). q(x1:T​∣x0​)=t=1∏T​q(xt​∣xt−1​).

Closed-form reparameterization:

q(xt∣x0)=N(αˉt x0, (1−αˉt)I), q(x_t \mid x_0) = \mathcal N(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0, \, (1-\bar\alpha_t) I), q(xt​∣x0​)=N(αˉt​​x0​,(1−αˉt​)I), xt=αˉt x0+1−αˉt ϵ,ϵ∼N(0,I). x_t = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\epsilon, \quad \epsilon \sim \mathcal N(0,I). xt​=αˉt​​x0​+1−αˉt​​ϵ,ϵ∼N(0,I).

3. Reverse Diffusion (Denoising Process)

We want to model the reverse chain:

pθ(x0:T)=p(xT)∏t=1Tpθ(xt−1∣xt),p_\theta(x_{0:T}) = p(x_T) \prod_{t=1}^T p_\theta(x_{t-1} \mid x_t),pθ​(x0:T​)=p(xT​)t=1∏T​pθ​(xt−1​∣xt​),

with Gaussian transitions:

pθ(xt−1∣xt)=N(μθ(xt,t),Σθ(xt,t)).p_\theta(x_{t-1} \mid x_t) = \mathcal N(\mu_\theta(x_t, t), \Sigma_\theta(x_t, t)).pθ​(xt−1​∣xt​)=N(μθ​(xt​,t),Σθ​(xt​,t)).

4. Variational Lower Bound (ELBO)

We lower bound the log-likelihood:

log⁡pθ(x0)≥Eq[log⁡pθ(x0:T)−log⁡q(x1:T∣x0)].\log p_\theta(x_0) \ge \mathbb E_q \left[ \log p_\theta(x_{0:T}) - \log q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0) \right].logpθ​(x0​)≥Eq​[logpθ​(x0:T​)−logq(x1:T​∣x0​)].

Expanding yields:

LVLB=KL(q(xT∣x0) ∥ p(xT))⏟LT+∑t=2TEq[KL(q(xt−1∣xt,x0) ∥ pθ(xt−1∣xt))]⏟Lt−Eq[log⁡pθ(x0∣x1)]⏟L0.\mathcal L_{\text{VLB}} = \underbrace{\mathrm{KL}(q(x_T \mid x_0) \,\|\, p(x_T))}_{L_T} + \sum_{t=2}^T \underbrace{\mathbb E_q \big[ \mathrm{KL}(q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0) \,\|\, p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)) \big]}_{L_t} - \underbrace{\mathbb E_q[\log p_\theta(x_0 \mid x_1)]}_{L_0}.LVLB​=LT​KL(q(xT​∣x0​)∥p(xT​))​​+t=2∑T​Lt​Eq​[KL(q(xt−1​∣xt​,x0​)∥pθ​(xt−1​∣xt​))]​​−L0​Eq​[logpθ​(x0​∣x1​)]​​.

5. The True Posterior

Since both forward and reverse are Gaussian, the posterior is closed-form:

q(xt−1∣xt,x0)=N(μ~t(xt,x0), β~tI),q(x_{t-1} \mid x_t, x_0) = \mathcal N(\tilde\mu_t(x_t, x_0), \, \tilde\beta_t I),q(xt−1​∣xt​,x0​)=N(μ~​t​(xt​,x0​),β~​t​I),

where

μ~t(xt,x0)=αˉt−1βt1−αˉt x0+αt(1−αˉt−1)1−αˉt xt,\tilde\mu_t(x_t,x_0) = \frac{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_{t-1}} \beta_t}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0 + \frac{\sqrt{\alpha_t}(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,x_t,μ~​t​(xt​,x0​)=1−αˉt​αˉt−1​​βt​​x0​+1−αˉt​αt​​(1−αˉt−1​)​xt​, β~t=1−αˉt−11−αˉt βt.\tilde\beta_t = \frac{1-\bar\alpha_{t-1}}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\beta_t.β~​t​=1−αˉt​1−αˉt−1​​βt​.

6. Simplifying KL Terms

With fixed variance (\Sigma_\theta(x_t,t)=\tilde\beta_t I), the KL reduces to a squared error:

Lt=Eq[12β~t∥μ~t(xt,x0)−μθ(xt,t)∥2]+const.L_t = \mathbb E_q \left[ \frac{1}{2\tilde\beta_t} \|\tilde\mu_t(x_t,x_0) - \mu_\theta(x_t,t)\|^2 \right] + \text{const}.Lt​=Eq​[2β~​t​1​∥μ~​t​(xt​,x0​)−μθ​(xt​,t)∥2]+const.

7. Noise Prediction Parameterization

Recall:

xt=αˉtx0+1−αˉtϵ.x_t = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t} x_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t} \epsilon.xt​=αˉt​​x0​+1−αˉt​​ϵ.

We rearrange to express (x_0):

x0=1αˉt(xt−1−αˉt ϵ).x_0 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}} \left(x_t - \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\epsilon \right).x0​=αˉt​​1​(xt​−1−αˉt​​ϵ).

Substituting into the posterior mean, we can define:

μθ(xt,t)=1αt(xt−1−αt1−αˉtϵθ(xt,t)).\mu_\theta(x_t,t) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\alpha_t}} \left(x_t - \frac{1-\alpha_t}{\sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}} \epsilon_\theta(x_t,t)\right).μθ​(xt​,t)=αt​​1​(xt​−1−αˉt​​1−αt​​ϵθ​(xt​,t)).

8. The Noise Prediction Loss

Now the KL term becomes:

Lt=Ex0,ϵ[αt22β~t(1−αˉt)∥ϵ−ϵθ(xt,t)∥2]+const.L_t = \mathbb E_{x_0,\epsilon} \left[ \frac{\alpha_t^2}{2\tilde\beta_t (1-\bar\alpha_t)} \|\epsilon - \epsilon_\theta(x_t,t)\|^2 \right] + \text{const}.Lt​=Ex0​,ϵ​[2β~​t​(1−αˉt​)αt2​​∥ϵ−ϵθ​(xt​,t)∥2]+const.

The prefactor depends only on the noise schedule.

Lsimple(θ)=Ex0,t,ϵ[∥ϵ−ϵθ(xt,t)∥2]\boxed{L_{\text{simple}}(\theta) = \mathbb E_{x_0, t, \epsilon} \big[ \|\epsilon - \epsilon_\theta(x_t, t)\|^2 \big]}Lsimple​(θ)=Ex0​,t,ϵ​[∥ϵ−ϵθ​(xt​,t)∥2]​

9. Sampling

After training, we generate by reversing the chain:

  1. Start with (x_T \sim \mathcal N(0,I)).
  2. Iteratively compute:
xt−1=μθ(xt,t)+σtz,z∼N(0,I).x_{t-1} = \mu_\theta(x_t, t) + \sigma_t z, \quad z\sim\mathcal N(0,I).xt−1​=μθ​(xt​,t)+σt​z,z∼N(0,I).
  1. After (T) steps, obtain (x_0).

10. Summary

  • Diffusion models are likelihood-based generative models.
  • Training uses a noise-prediction objective, but it is mathematically equivalent to maximizing a variational lower bound on (\log p_\theta(x_0)).
  • The trick: reformulate MLE into a simple regression problem of predicting Gaussian noise.
