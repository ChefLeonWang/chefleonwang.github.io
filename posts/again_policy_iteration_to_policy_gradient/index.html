Again into policy iteration and policy gradient: A Deep Learning Analogy

Published: 22 May, 2025 |  at  04:30 AM
| Suggest Changes

🎯 Motivation

In reinforcement learning, policy iteration and policy gradient methods offer two distinct strategies for learning control policies. Interestingly, these two paradigms resemble the evolution in machine learning from shallow models with exact solvers to deep learning with gradient-based optimization.

  • Policy Iteration ↔ Classical Optimization (e.g., SVM, logistic regression)
  • Policy Gradient ↔ Deep Learning (SGD)

🔁 Policy Iteration

Policy iteration consists of two steps:

  1. Policy Evaluation: Estimate the value function Vπ(s)V^{\pi}(s)Vπ(s) for a given policy π\piπ:
Vπ(s)=Eπ[∑t=0∞γtr(st,at)∣s0=s]V^{\pi}(s) = \mathbb{E}_\pi \left[ \sum_{t=0}^\infty \gamma^t r(s_t, a_t) \mid s_0 = s \right] Vπ(s)=Eπ​[t=0∑∞​γtr(st​,at​)∣s0​=s]

This typically involves solving the Bellman equation:

Vπ(s)=∑aπ(a∣s)[R(s,a)+γ∑s′P(s′∣s,a)Vπ(s′)]V^{\pi}(s) = \sum_a \pi(a \mid s) \left[ R(s,a) + \gamma \sum_{s'} P(s' \mid s, a) V^{\pi}(s') \right] Vπ(s)=a∑​π(a∣s)[R(s,a)+γs′∑​P(s′∣s,a)Vπ(s′)]
  1. Policy Improvement: Compute a new policy by acting greedily w.r.t. Qπ(s,a)Q^{\pi}(s,a)Qπ(s,a):
π′(s)=arg⁡max⁡aQπ(s,a)\pi'(s) = \arg\max_a Q^{\pi}(s,a) π′(s)=argamax​Qπ(s,a)

Repeat until convergence.

🧠 Policy Gradient

Policy gradient methods directly optimize the parameters of a stochastic policy πθ(a∣s)\pi_\theta(a \mid s)πθ​(a∣s):

Objective:

J(θ)=Eτ∼πθ[∑t=0∞γtr(st,at)]J(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{\tau \sim \pi_\theta} \left[ \sum_{t=0}^\infty \gamma^t r(s_t, a_t) \right] J(θ)=Eτ∼πθ​​[t=0∑∞​γtr(st​,at​)]

Policy Gradient Theorem:

∇θJ(θ)=Es∼dπ,a∼πθ[∇θlog⁡πθ(a∣s)Qπ(s,a)]\nabla_\theta J(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{s \sim d^{\pi}, a \sim \pi_\theta} \left[ \nabla_\theta \log \pi_\theta(a \mid s) Q^{\pi}(s,a) \right] ∇θ​J(θ)=Es∼dπ,a∼πθ​​[∇θ​logπθ​(a∣s)Qπ(s,a)]

This enables gradient ascent using SGD-like updates:

θ←θ+α∇θJ(θ)\theta \leftarrow \theta + \alpha \nabla_\theta J(\theta) θ←θ+α∇θ​J(θ)

This avoids hard arg⁡max⁡\arg\maxargmax, allowing optimization over high-dimensional, continuous action spaces.

📈 Classical Optimization (Shallow ML)

Consider logistic regression or linear regression with a closed-form solution.

🔁 Deep Learning and Stochastic Gradient Descent

In deep learning, we train a neural network parameterized by θ\thetaθ using stochastic gradient descent:

θ←θ−η∇θL(θ)\theta \leftarrow \theta - \eta \nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}(\theta) θ←θ−η∇θ​L(θ)

Where L(θ)\mathcal{L}(\theta)L(θ) is typically:

L(θ)=1N∑i=1Nℓ(fθ(xi),yi)\mathcal{L}(\theta) = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^N \ell(f_\theta(x_i), y_i) L(θ)=N1​i=1∑N​ℓ(fθ​(xi​),yi​)
  • No closed-form solution
  • Non-convex landscape
  • Relies entirely on gradient signals

Just like policy gradient, this process improves the model locally, one step at a time.

🧠 Summary

  • Policy iteration is exact but limited to small, discrete spaces.
  • Policy gradient is flexible, scalable, and essential for neural policies.
  • This mirrors ML’s shift from closed-form optimization to scalable SGD.

Just as deep learning replaced exact solvers in high-dimensional spaces, policy gradient replaced greedy iterations with continuous learning.
