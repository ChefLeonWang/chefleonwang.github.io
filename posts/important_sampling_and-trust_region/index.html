üîß Clipping in PPO

Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) avoids the complexities of second-order optimization (like TRPO) by using a clipped objective to restrict the size of policy updates.

üîπ The Motivation

Directly maximizing the surrogate objective using importance sampling:

Et[rt(Œ∏‚Ä≤)A^t],wherert(Œ∏‚Ä≤)=œÄŒ∏‚Ä≤(at‚à£st)œÄŒ∏(at‚à£st)\mathbb{E}_t \left[ r_t(\theta') \hat{A}_t \right], \quad \text{where} \quad r_t(\theta') = \frac{\pi_{\theta'}(a_t|s_t)}{\pi_\theta(a_t|s_t)} Et‚Äã[rt‚Äã(Œ∏‚Ä≤)A^t‚Äã],wherert‚Äã(Œ∏‚Ä≤)=œÄŒ∏‚Äã(at‚Äã‚à£st‚Äã)œÄŒ∏‚Ä≤‚Äã(at‚Äã‚à£st‚Äã)‚Äã

can lead to large updates when rt(Œ∏‚Ä≤)r_t(\theta')rt‚Äã(Œ∏‚Ä≤) deviates too far from 1.

To prevent this, PPO introduces the clipped surrogate objective:

üî∏ The PPO Objective

LCLIP(Œ∏‚Ä≤)=Et[min‚Å°(rt(Œ∏‚Ä≤)A^t,clip(rt(Œ∏‚Ä≤),1‚àíœµ,1+œµ)A^t)]L^{\text{CLIP}}(\theta') = \mathbb{E}_t \left[ \min \left( r_t(\theta') \hat{A}_t, \text{clip}(r_t(\theta'), 1 - \epsilon, 1 + \epsilon) \hat{A}_t \right) \right] LCLIP(Œ∏‚Ä≤)=Et‚Äã[min(rt‚Äã(Œ∏‚Ä≤)A^t‚Äã,clip(rt‚Äã(Œ∏‚Ä≤),1‚àíœµ,1+œµ)A^t‚Äã)]

This objective:

  • Encourages improvement when rt‚âà1r_t \approx 1rt‚Äã‚âà1
  • Suppresses updates that would push rtr_trt‚Äã outside of [1‚àíœµ,1+œµ][1 - \epsilon, 1 + \epsilon][1‚àíœµ,1+œµ]

üîπ Why It Works

  • The min‚Å°\minmin operator ensures that the improvement is monotonic and avoids overshooting
  • Clipping creates a flat region in the loss around rt=1r_t = 1rt‚Äã=1, leading to more stable gradients
  • It implicitly controls KL divergence without computing it directly

üîπ Visual Intuition

  • When A^t>0\hat{A}_t > 0A^t‚Äã>0: we don‚Äôt want rt‚â´1r_t \gg 1rt‚Äã‚â´1 to overly favor a good action ‚Äî clipping caps the advantage
  • When A^t<0\hat{A}_t < 0A^t‚Äã<0: we don‚Äôt want rt‚â™1r_t \ll 1rt‚Äã‚â™1 to overly punish ‚Äî clipping limits how far you move away

In both cases, clipping avoids large policy changes ‚Äî achieving the same stability goal as TRPO, but with simpler first-order methods.

üîπ Typical Values

  • œµ‚àà[0.1,0.3]\epsilon \in [0.1, 0.3]œµ‚àà[0.1,0.3] ‚Äî common values in PPO implementations
  • Used with adaptive learning rate and early stopping by KL threshold to further stabilize updates
