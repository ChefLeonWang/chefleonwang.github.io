Beyond KL: Other Divergences

Published: 1 Sep, 2025 |  at  04:30 AM
| Suggest Changes

Understanding the Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence is foundational in machine learning, but it is not the only way to measure the distance between probability distributions. Depending on the scenario, several other divergence or distance metrics may be more appropriate. This blog post surveys major alternatives, their definitions, properties, and usage in machine learning.

1. Total Variation Distance (TV)

Definition: TV(P,Q)=12∑x∣P(x)−Q(x)∣TV(P, Q) = \frac{1}{2} \sum_x |P(x) - Q(x)|TV(P,Q)=21​∑x​∣P(x)−Q(x)∣

Properties:

  • Metric ✅
  • Symmetric ✅
  • Bounded between 0 and 1
  • Measures maximum difference in assigned probabilities

Applications:

  • Theoretical analysis in PAC learning
  • Fairness, privacy, and robustness

2. Jensen-Shannon Divergence (JSD)

Definition: JS(P∥Q)=12DKL(P∥M)+12DKL(Q∥M),M=12(P+Q)JS(P \parallel Q) = \frac{1}{2} D_{KL}(P \parallel M) + \frac{1}{2} D_{KL}(Q \parallel M), \quad M = \frac{1}{2}(P + Q)JS(P∥Q)=21​DKL​(P∥M)+21​DKL​(Q∥M),M=21​(P+Q)

Properties:

  • Symmetric ✅
  • Bounded between 0 and log⁡2\log 2log2
  • Square root defines a metric

Applications:

  • Used in GANs (e.g., original GAN by Goodfellow et al., 2014)
  • Language modeling and diversity measures

3. Wasserstein Distance (Earth Mover’s Distance)

Definition (1D case): W(P,Q)=inf⁡γ∈Π(P,Q)E(x,y)∼γ[∥x−y∥]W(P, Q) = \inf_{\gamma \in \Pi(P, Q)} \mathbb{E}_{(x, y) \sim \gamma}[\|x - y\|]W(P,Q)=infγ∈Π(P,Q)​E(x,y)∼γ​[∥x−y∥]

Where Π(P,Q)\Pi(P, Q)Π(P,Q) is the set of all joint distributions with marginals P and Q.

Properties:

  • Metric ✅
  • Defined even for disjoint supports ❗
  • Captures geometry of distributions

Applications:

  • Wasserstein GANs (WGAN)
  • Domain adaptation
  • Out-of-distribution detection

Properties:

  • Symmetric ✅
  • Metric ✅
  • Bounded between 0 and 1

  • Variational inference
  • Theoretical guarantees

5. Chi-Squared Divergence

Definition: χ2(P∥Q)=∑x(P(x)−Q(x))2Q(x)\chi^2(P \parallel Q) = \sum_x \frac{(P(x) - Q(x))^2}{Q(x)}χ2(P∥Q)=∑x​Q(x)(P(x)−Q(x))2​

Properties:

  • Not symmetric ❌
  • Very sensitive to small Q(x) values ❗

Applications:

  • Second-order information analysis
  • Gradient dynamics studies

6. f-Divergence Family

Many divergences, including KL, JS, TV, and Chi-Squared, are instances of the f-divergence family:

7. Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD)

Definition: MMD(P,Q)=sup⁡f∈F(EP[f(x)]−EQ[f(x)])\text{MMD}(P, Q) = \sup_{f \in \mathcal{F}} \left( \mathbb{E}_{P}[f(x)] - \mathbb{E}_{Q}[f(x)] \right)MMD(P,Q)=supf∈F​(EP​[f(x)]−EQ​[f(x)])

Where F\mathcal{F}F is a unit ball in a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS).

  • Two-sample tests
  • Representation learning
  • MMD GANs
